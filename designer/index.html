1967.4.24 Soyuz 1

Parachute Failure

The one-day mission had been plagued by a series of mishaps with the new spacecraft type, culminating with its parachute not opening properly after atmospheric reentry. Komarov was killed when the capsule hit the ground at high speed.

1971.6.30 Soyuz 11

Decompression

The crew of Soyuz 11 were killed after undocking from space station Salyut 1 after a three-week stay. A cabin vent valve accidentally opened at service module separation. The recovery team found the crew dead. These are the only fatalities in space (above 100 kilometres (62 mi)) thus far.

1986.1.28 Challenger

Vehicle disintegration during launch

Space Shuttle Challenger disintegrates 73 seconds after its 1986 launch, due to hot gases escaping through a damaged seal in an SRB leading to structural failure of the external tank. The accident resulted in the death of all seven crew members.

2003.2.1 Columbia

Vehicle disintegration on re-entry

The Space Shuttle Columbia was lost as it returned from a two-week mission, STS-107. Damage to the shuttle's thermal protection system (TPS) led to structural failure of the shuttle's left wing and the spacecraft ultimately broke apart.The vehicle broke up over the southwestern United States and fell in fragments over eastern Texas and central Louisiana.
